Easter Term 2014 Events

Saturday, 31st May, 10:30am: CGS Connect

The topic of our Connect meeting is "Can academic work become an idol?". Do we sometimes value our academic work more than God? What is challenging about setting our priorities right, especially during busy times? Come along and join us at CGS Connect this Saturday for a discussion of academic work as an idol and how it relates to our relationship with God. We will look at some Bible passages, and think about how to focus on God in our work and honour Him with it. Light refreshments will be provided.

Venue: Sidney Sussex College Chapel

Saturday, 14th June, 10:30am: CGS Connect

Join us for a session of worship, music, and prayer.

In his classic "Knowing God", Dr Jim Packer writes that "people who know their God are before anything else people who pray." When we pray with others, we can be strengthened as we trust and commit ourselves to the Lord who Hears. Come along and join us at CGS Connect this Saturday for a prayer and worship session. Join us in prayer for the Graduate Community in Cambridge and be an encouragement for each other in songs of worship. We have selected some of the finest Christian songs and hymns, which will be accompanied by piano, flute and guitar music.

Venue: Latimer Room, Clare College

Saturday, 3rd May, 10:30am: CGS Connect

The topic of our connect this Saturday is "A Place to Ask Your Hard Questions".

It’s a new term, a new season and CGS has a new leadership committee, so we thought it fitting to try something a little different with this Connect. We want CGS to be a community of postgraduates committed to excellence in Christian scholarship and to being salt and light within the university. We also want CGS to be a welcoming place for both believers and those who are exploring Christian faith. In being a place for believers to not have to pretend they have it all figured out, we wanted to give room for even the most mature believer to talk about any hard questions or stumbling blocks in their faith journey. We would be heartbroken if the demands of the secular university resulted in any Christian student abandoning their faith rather than growing in it. We’ve designed this Connect for anyone, including seekers, to come and ask questions. We can’t promise to answer all of them in one morning, but we do want to begin and we hope you can join us. If you have questions you’d like to talk about, but can’t come on Saturday, please feel free at any time to get in touch with a CGS committee member or CGS staff worker, Paula Puckett at ppuckett@go.mailsvc.com. For those that can join us, there will be space to ask questions out loud or by submitting them on paper. If you’d like to ask a question ahead of time, please email one of us. Please remember that the person of Jesus can withstand the hardest questions and biggest obstacles.

Time: 10.30am - 12:00pm

Venue: Latimer Room, Clare College
